MSVC, clang and some GCC versions detect warnings when using `BOOST_NO_EXCEPTIONS` around here at lines [1810](https://github.com/boostorg/multiprecision/blob/a4cd4ffe2f4c008e0fb2cdeeae1ff2a8c62efe51/include/boost/multiprecision/cpp_int.hpp#L1810), [1846](https://github.com/boostorg/multiprecision/blob/a4cd4ffe2f4c008e0fb2cdeeae1ff2a8c62efe51/include/boost/multiprecision/cpp_int.hpp#L1846) and [1884](https://github.com/boostorg/multiprecision/blob/a4cd4ffe2f4c008e0fb2cdeeae1ff2a8c62efe51/include/boost/multiprecision/cpp_int.hpp#L1884).  
  
I'm a bit surprised I missed these while ramping up for 1.80. Sorry I missed them at that time.  
  
There are a few ways to fix these. Either initialize the variable `val` at its inception, or initialize it within the places where the exception stuff expands to nothing.  
  
I would initialize `val` in the scopes where the exception stuff expands to nothing. Does anyone have a different preference of how to fix them?  
  
Otherwise, I'll fix them and PR the changes ASAP.
