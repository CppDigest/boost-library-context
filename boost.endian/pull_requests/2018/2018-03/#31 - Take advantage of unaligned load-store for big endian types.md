I noticed that the generated assembly for unaligned big endian types wasn't nearly as clean as it should be for power of 2 sized integers.  I tried a few different approaches, but ultimately it appears that replicating the optimization from `{load,store}_little_endian()` results in the least code changes and results in optimal assembly output.  
  
I'm curious whether or not this optimization can be opened up to more platforms, since from my testing, this change turns the unaligned big endian types on x86 into zero overhead abstractions.
