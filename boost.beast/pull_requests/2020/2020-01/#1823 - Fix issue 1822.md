Anyway what is the necessity for being so verbose? Why not just call `make_strand` at the call site of `make_shared<session>`? Also the comment adds no value, the reader already know's C++, they know a delegating constructor is being called.

> Username: madmongo1

Calling make_strand at the call site passes responsibility for thread-safety to the caller. I find this philosophically concerning as it's leaking information.  
  
To be honest, the program does not actually require strands at all. There are no concurrent async operations in play.  
  
I wonder whether we'd be better off removing all mention of strands altogether.

> Username: vinniefalco  
> Created_at: 2020-01-27 07:25:13 UTC  
> Updated_at: 2020-01-27 13:26:46 UTC  
> Url: https://github.com/boostorg/beast/pull/1823#discussion_r371090454  

> Calling make_strand at the call site passes responsibility for thread-safety to the caller.  
  
Yes, that's exactly right. The caller is the one with the information on whether or not the strand is necessary, since only the caller knows how many threads are calling `io_context::run`.  
  
> the program does not actually require strands at all.  
  
It doesn't, but the philosophy of the examples is to always include them so that users have more robust code in the face of changes. 90% of crashing bug reports are caused by missing strands in multi-threaded contexts.

> Username: vinniefalco  
> Created_at: 2020-01-27 07:32:15 UTC  
> Updated_at: 2020-01-27 13:26:46 UTC  
> Url: https://github.com/boostorg/beast/pull/1823#discussion_r371092128  

If anything, we should either include a very brief comment on why we're using strands, and/or include a few sentences in the documentation about the use of strands in the examples. And we should audit every example and make sure they are using strands consistently. I agree that we want to transition from mentioning the `io_context` executor to the `strand` as early as possible. Anything that we do with respect to in-code comments, has to be _brief_ and copied to all examples.
