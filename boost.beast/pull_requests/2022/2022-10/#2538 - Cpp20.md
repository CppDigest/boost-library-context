  55 |+     // These objects perform our I/O

  57 |+     auto resolver = net::use_awaitable.as_default_on(tcp::resolver(co_await net::this_coro::executor));

> Username: yuvalif  
> Created_at: 2022-10-19 14:38:07 UTC  
> Updated_at: 2022-10-19 14:38:08 UTC  
> Url: https://github.com/boostorg/beast/pull/2538#discussion_r999554813  

what is the benefit of creating the resolver in this way compared to:  
```cpp  
tcp::resolver resolver(ioc);  
```  
and then invoking the coroutine with `net::use_awaitable` as an argument?  
```cpp  
co_await resolver.async_resolve(host, port, net::use_awaitable);  
```

> Username: klemens-morgenstern  
> Created_at: 2022-10-20 02:09:46 UTC  
> Url: https://github.com/boostorg/beast/pull/2538#discussion_r1000074914  

Brevity.

> Username: vinniefalco  
> Created_at: 2022-10-27 14:44:46 UTC  
> Url: https://github.com/boostorg/beast/pull/2538#discussion_r1006974919  

No, not just brevity - passing `io_context` around is a code smell. You should be thinking and writing in terms of executors not contexts. That line would be better if it read:  
```  
tcp::resolver resolver( ioc.get_executor() );  
```  
  
Even better would be to write `make_executor` and call that from everywhere, then you can switch to a strand easily by changing one function.
